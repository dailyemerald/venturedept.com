WHAT IF WE TREATED EVERYTHING THE WAY WE TREAT SEX?

CLIENT: University of Oregon Health Center

AMBITION: Produce a promotional video to increase downloads of the Health Center's new sex education app, SexPositive.

SERVICES: Writing, video production, design, boner jokes

In order to better inform its students, the University of Oregon Health Center knew that it would have to communicate with students in a way that interested and engaged them in a relevant way. In the true Oregon spirit of innovation, it did something that very few other university health centers have ever attempted: The UO Health Center created an app called SexPositive.

The app uses a fun roulette system to educate students on how to have safe sex, minimize exposure to STIs, and communicate consent to partners. The Health Center tasked The Venture Dept. with creating a promotion that engaged students as well as the app itself, so we created a video that students would love to share on social media. Our video asks: "What if we treated every experience like we treat sex?"

By poking fun at some of the absurd (and ill-informed) ways that sex is talked about on college campuses, we attempted to change the social climate surrounding casual student-to-student discussions about STIs, consent, and safe sex. The video reached over 2500 views in one month, prompting a major spike in the appâ€™s downloads. By leveraging humor, sex, and the skills of our production team, we were able to spread the word about the app and help the UO Health Center positively affect a larger portion of the student population.

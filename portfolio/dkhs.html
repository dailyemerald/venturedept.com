EVERY DUCK NEEDS A GREAT NEST TO CALL HOME

CLIENT: DucksHousing.com, a product of Emerald Media Group

AMBITION: Leverage a revamp of the DucksHousing.com identity and use it to create both a printed Housing Guide and a promotional campaign.

SERVICES: Design, social media, web development, writing, photography

Within Emerald Media Group is a department called The Garage, a place where students throw technology and big ideas together to see what results. One project that grew from The Garage is DucksHousing.com, a website that aggregates rental units from property management companies in Eugene. It has grown to prominence in the online property management sphere for University of Oregon students, but we knew that it could be even better. That’s how the Venture Dept. got involved.

Starting from a simple visual refresh of the site, (new colors, typography, and iconography) we created new assets and giveaways for the brand. This included a circular illustration of Eugene that was turned into posters and magnets for giveaways at DucksHousing.com events and a banner/tablecloth combination to improve the event presentation.

In addition to improving the brand-to-student relationships in person through events, we took to social media to broaden our reach. We knew that college students value very little over pizza, so we did what seemed perfectly natural: Started holding twitter contests every week with the winner receiving hot pizzas delivered right to their doors. All the students had to do was tweet our hashtag #FindYourHome and a specific URL that outlined contest instructions. To improve entries, we built a custom twitter card that would automatically pop up when the URL was used on twitter. The contest was a success and has increased the DucksHousing.com twitter follower count by over 30 percent, in addition to driving traffic to the site.

For many years, the Emerald newsroom created an annual series of housing guides that showcased Eugene neighborhoods and gave advice about how to find the perfect off-campus home. Since The Venture Dept. had become heavily involved with the DucksHousing.com brand, we decided to expand our services to include longform content creation by taking over the annual housing guides. The final product was inserted into the Emerald and handed out at 160 distribution points to a 5,500 circulation over the course of four days.
